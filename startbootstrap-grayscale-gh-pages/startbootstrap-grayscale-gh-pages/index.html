About Me

I'm a design professional with 5 years of experience in graphics and front-end website content. I am an advanced Adobe Suite user, and I can navigate complex software. I got my start by doing set designs for live theater while obtaining a Bachelor of Arts in technical theater. After graduating, I ran lights and assisted with set up and strike for live concerts and comedy shows at an established casino venue until it was closed down. I then managed a small team of security professionals at an airport checkpoint while obtaining a Bachelor of Science in computer science. My hobbies have always been creative ones, and I have experience with various types of multimedia including mold making, screen printing, photography, 3D, music, and painting. I am currently seeking to get back into hands-on, creative work.

Projects

Theater projects:

Set Design for Banker's Dozen 2009

Banker's Dozen was a student theater project performed in a small room without a budget. It was a comical play about banking. I utilized the university's underground prop storage reserve to select realistic banking furniture. I also added cartoonish details to lighten up the mood like a giant, green dollar sign painted on the floor and foam lion statues that I spray-painted gold for a metallic appearance. It was a memorable delight to see the performers doing coordinated dance routines around the dollar sign.

Set Design for Waiting for Lefty 2010

Waiting for Lefty was another student theater project, but it was given a small budget and the university's main theater room. It is a historically accurate play about a taxi union dispute. I was given a raised platform layout and back wall, so I placed them center stage and added a large printout of the taxis that were involved in the play. Additionally, I added historically accurate wainscotting that I painted to match the wall, and I posted enlarged newspaper headlines sourced from historical archives on the platform.

Event technician set up at Ameristar

An example of the physical and technical labor I assisted with at a theater located in a casino called Ameristar. As a technician, I was tasked with safely moving the large wooden tables alone, coiling and uncoiling sound system wires into place, running spotlights, and organizing seating.
